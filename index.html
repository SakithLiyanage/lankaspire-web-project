The history of tea leaf in the world

Ceylon cinnamon, originating from Sri Lanka, boasts a history dating back over 2,000 years. Highly prized for its unique flavor and aromatic qualities, this spice found its way along ancient trade routes, including the Silk Road. As demand surged, European powers such as the Portuguese and Dutch vied for control. The Dutch eventually established a cinnamon monopoly in the 16th century, tightly regulating production in Ceylon.

The allure of Ceylon cinnamon extended beyond culinary uses, with the spice being valued for its potential medicinal properties. Today, Ceylon cinnamon retains its reputation as the "true cinnamon," distinct from the more common cassia varieties, and continues to be celebrated for its rich history and exceptional quality in both traditional and modern applications.
